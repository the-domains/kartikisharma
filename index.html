A Memoir of Travels

BlogInstagram

Up is down. Day becomes night. Rays reveal the stars. They also release an unforgettable serenity that is watching a Southern California sunset. Huntington Beach on a November evening lays quiet and bare, an invisible hand playing with shades and luminance. Almost as if watching a live painting. The gentleness of the deep blue, the never-ending laps, and the faint whisper of a wind make for a master record – listening to the ocean from land.

This was captured on a Nikon Coolpix S7000.

Los Angeles

HUNTINGTON BEACH, PACIFIC COAST HIGHWAY

Situated in the Rhine Rift Valley, Heidelberg is a quaint river town with history rooted among the times of the Celts and the “Mountain of Saints.” Now known as a college town bustling with a younger crowd, it surprisingly carries a relaxed atmosphere with soft conversations. With its blend of architectural styles – Gothic to Renaissance – and a warm color palette complimenting its clear summer sky, sit back and enjoy the sweet repose. Or indulge in a path on foot for the smaller stories of centuries past.

Miami

MARJORY STONEMAN DOUGLAS OCEAN BEACH PARK, SOUTH BEACH

On the western edge of the country, the Marin Headlands are a refuge for the philosopher’s thoughts. Find a seat, pick a cloud, and lock secrets in the traveling sky. One of the most memorable drives, this road captures a different frame every second. Whether the path winds up or down, there is a hug at every curve and that adrenaline rush every time it may lead into the ocean for a swim. This was captured on the iPhone 6.

San Francisco

MARIN HEADLANDS

Transporting to other worlds begins with the desire to create an alternate reality. How do we even learn about this possibility? Imagination. And in our world, we are fortunately surrounded by those gifted with the written word, introducing ideas and genuinely inspiring. J.K. Rowling and her pen created Tom Riddle’s diary and introduced little minds to the blurred lines surrounding this eternal duel between good and evil. We distinguish light from dark, pick our sides, and if we do not know, we hope for that little lumos in the heart. The wonder and magic from Harry’s first visit to Ollivander’s was preserved in between these wand boxes, behind each door, and on this balcony. Thank you, with the magic of love and a million wands & over, to John Hurt for embodying the Garrick Ollivander in your most wondrous light. This was captured on the iPhone 6.

Austria

SWAROVSKI KRISTALLWELTEN

Just outside of Innsbruck, the Swarovski Kristallwelten in Wattens is nestled in a green valley. Almost cryptic, the entrance to the caves of this world already dazzles with crystal eyes. Inside the commemorative museum, each space and exhibit is designed with little beauties like this one. This one, in particular, drew me in with that ocean blue – why stop the otherworldliness at the caves resting on the feet of mountains when shades like this one can transport you just as well? This was captured on the Nikon Coolpix S60.

On a drive through the arrondissements in Paris, the summer nights house soft conversations. This city has stories short and tall, the walls of yesteryear speak of the grandiose, and the aesthetic of French brush lines can be seen in its intricate design and beautiful architecture. This was captured on the Nikon Coolpix S60.

There’s that glow again. This time, Chicago is serving up a grand night with its blanketing wind and towering reflections. A drive down State Street on a busy night reveals the dancing lights. A walk down any street means walking to a rhythm, a soundtrack composed of melody, drama, traffic, phone calls, steps…life. Chicago surrounds you and there is no hug quite like this one. This was captured from my iPhone 7 on State Street, just before crossing the Chicago River.

Vancouver

Cordova Street

The warmth of nightlights continues over in Vancouver, where the Waterfront Station rests like a fireplace on a winter evening. The city is a wonderful walk in its entirety and a delight to the heart – defined by age-old details and homeliness unique to this air. This was taken on my iPhone 6 from the walkway bridge overlooking Cordova and Granville, only minutes away from a seat near the harbour.

Located across the street from the Wiener Staatsoper, Hotel Bristol is an architectural delight in the evening. An autumn evening in Wien seems to compliment the warmth and elegance of this structure, the lines echoing the history of the city throughout each of the palace grounds. This was captured on my iPhone 6 from the steps of the Staatsoper, while I spent what seemed like an hour waiting for the night lights to turn on and of course, to people watch. This is one of my favorite nights ever. Perhaps because I am biased with my love for the city or perhaps, because for the first time in a long time, the sweet familiar sound of a violin found my soul.
